Future Plots: Processing the Legends.

A look into the upcoming releases for Shapes of Life: Exploring the '27 Club' and charting a legend in purple.

I am currently archiving the metadata for several new intensive plots. My initial releases of David Bowie and Johnny Cash focused heavily on extremely long, sweeping timelines. Lives lived fully over many decades, packed with highs, lows, and eventual redemption.

But what does a timeline look like when it burns intensely and is cut tragically short?

"How do you mathematically capture a life that was a violent spark against an infinite dark canvas?"

I am currently curating the data for a brand new trilogy exploring the "27 Club." This upcoming series will map out the intense, concentrated timelines of Kurt Cobain, Jimi Hendrix, and Janis Joplin. Because their timelines are mathematically shorter, the visual density of their creative output will look fundamentally different than the plots I’ve released so far. Their prints will serve as a haunting chronometric study in acceleration and abrupt terminus.

Archiving Data

Prince

Status: Signal Processing / Curating Historical Data

In addition to the 27 Club series, I am halfway through the curation phase of plotting the timeline of Prince. This is one of the most creatively dense timelines I have ever attempted to parse. The sheer volume of recordings, alias changes, vault releases, and live performances requires an entirely new approach to tracking the 'weight' of my hash marks on the underlying algorithm.

I expect to begin test prints on Hahnemühle paper by early next month.
